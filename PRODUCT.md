# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Primary**: Hiring managers, technical leads, and founders evaluating a full-stack / AI engineer for freelance or contract engagements. They arrive via LinkedIn, GitHub, X, or direct referral links, often on desktop during work hours, sometimes on mobile between meetings.
- **Secondary**: Fellow developers and potential collaborators browsing for inspiration or partnership. They tend to view source, inspect animations, and read case studies in depth.
- **Context**: Visitors are making a quick credibility judgment. They want to see proof of technical depth, taste, and reliability within the first 30 seconds.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to present Dilukshan Niranjan's work, case studies, writing, certifications, and contact details in a format that is itself evidence of craft. The site is both a container for content and a demonstration of the skills it advertises: performance-focused frontend engineering, motion design, AI-assisted product building, and attention to detail.

Success looks like:
- A visitor understands the practitioner's range (full-stack, AI, data engineering, systems) within one scroll.
- Case studies are read, not just scanned.
- Contact inquiries arrive with context already established.
- Peers reference the site as an example of technical-polish balance.

## Brand Personality

**Editorial, kinetic, precise.**

- **Voice**: Confident but not arrogant. Technical terms are used precisely, never performatively.
- **Tone**: Warm when personal, rigorous when describing work. No hype, no em-dashes, no filler intros.
- **Emotional goals**: Inspire confidence in technical ability. Signal that details matter. Leave a memorable impression of craft without ostentation.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS templates**: Big number / small label hero metrics, identical icon-heading-text card grids, gradient-text accents, side-stripe borders. These signal "template," not craft.
- **Standard portfolio grids**: Thumbnail + title + tag lists that treat work as content to be browsed rather than cases to be read.
- **Dark-blue developer cliché**: The "observability dashboard" reflex palette. This site should not be guessable from the category name alone.
- **Decoration without purpose**: Motion, WebGL, or glass effects that do not serve content reveal or narrative progression.

## Design Principles

1. **The medium is the message.** The site itself demonstrates the craft it sells. Performance, accessibility, and animation quality are part of the portfolio, not afterthoughts.
2. **Motion serves narrative.** Every animation should guide attention, reveal content, or signal state change. If it can be removed without losing meaning, it should be.
3. **Precision over novelty.** Polish existing patterns rather than inventing new ones for their own sake. A well-timed ease-out-quart says more than a bespoke physics simulation.
4. **Show depth, not breadth.** Case studies and writing should prove expertise in specific domains, not list every technology ever touched.
5. **Respect the visitor's time.** Fast loads, clear paths, readable type, and reduced-motion alternatives are non-negotiable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** compliance target.
- **Reduced motion**: All scroll-driven and entrance animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.
- **Focus management**: Skip link, visible focus indicators, and logical tab order on every page.
- **Color**: Sufficient contrast for all text and interactive elements. Avoid relying on color alone to convey meaning.
- **Cognitive load**: No auto-playing media. No surprise motion. Content hierarchy should be scannable without animation.
